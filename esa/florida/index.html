ESA in Florida

Florida families can use ESA funds for homeschool curriculum.

At ~$8,000/year via the PEP program, your ESA covers curriculum, tutoring, testing, and more. Wizkoo is $499/year — one line item, not the whole budget.

As of April 2026

Program details
Florida's school choice ecosystem — PEP is the one for homeschool families.
Which program is right for homeschoolers?
Florida has four school choice programs. PEP (Personal Education Program) is the true ESA for homeschool and micro-school families. FES-EO and FES-FTC primarily fund private school tuition. FES-UA serves students with IEPs. If you homeschool in Florida, PEP is your program. Note: PEP is at capacity for 2026-27 and is not accepting new applications this cycle.

FES-UA — For students with IEPs (disabilities)
Additional Funding (Disabilities)
FES-UA: up to $34,000+/year for ESE Level 5 students. ESE 1–3 ~$10K; ESE 4 ~$22K; ESE 5 ~$34K. $50,000 maximum account balance cap. Requires an IEP, no income limits.
Using your Florida PEP funds for Wizkoo
One line item in your PEP account.

Wizkoo is a personalized weekly learning platform for families with children ages 2 to 12. One theme connects every subject. Every child at the table gets a plan matched to their age and reading level.

At $50/month or $499/year, Wizkoo fits within Florida's PEP funding for homeschool families.

Wizkoo is not yet listed on the PEP marketplace. We're in the approval process and will update this page when approved.

In the meantime, families in states that allow direct reimbursement for educational software subscriptions may be able to use ESA funds for Wizkoo by submitting a receipt. Check with Step Up For Students before purchasing.
