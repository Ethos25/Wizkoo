Why this exists

You already knew

You knew before you had the language for it. Before the research confirmed it. Before anyone in your life agreed with you.

They don't see him.

Maybe it was the morning your daughter finished the worksheet in three minutes and spent the rest of the hour with her head on the desk, waiting. Maybe it was the call from the teacher who said your son needed to stop moving so much, as if moving was the problem. Maybe she came home one day and something behind her eyes was different, and you never got the full story, and you didn't need it. Maybe the school called him "too much" and "not enough" in the same sentence, and you sat in the parking lot afterward and thought: they don't see him.

Good isn't the same thing as alive.

Or maybe none of that happened. Maybe the school was fine. The teachers were kind. The grades were good. And you looked at your child and thought: good isn't the same thing as alive.

You built it yourself.

Or maybe you never put them in at all. Maybe you looked at the system before your oldest was born and thought: I can do this differently. Not because something was broken. Because you believed something better was possible and you were willing to build it yourself.

We don't ask why.

Every family that homeschools has a reason. Some left. Some never entered. Some are still figuring out what to call it. We don't ask why. We don't need to.

Some of us left something. Some of us chose something. All of us sat down on a Sunday night and tried to figure out what Monday was supposed to look like. And then we built it from scratch.

The methodology

One themeEvery subject

We call it Orbital Learning. One idea at the center, and every school subject orbits it, the way planets orbit a single sun. A broken bone becomes a week of anatomy, history, geography, writing, and math. A backyard turtle becomes biology, art, cartography, and a letter to a wildlife center. The starting point is not a scope and sequence. The starting point is your life.

Most curricula teach subjects in parallel lines that never cross. Reading at 9:00. Math at 10:00. Science at 11:00. The child learns facts in isolation and is expected to build the connections alone.
Four children studying the same volcano get four different plans. Same table. Same morning. Nobody is waiting for anyone
Age 2
Explorer
Sensory play. Texture, color, sound.

Guided exploration. Names, shapes, stories.

Week 8 references Week 3. What your child learned in January shows up in March. The system does not start over. It remembers.

Orbital Learning is built on 30 years of integrated thematic instruction research from Stanford and Columbia, designed by educators, and made accessible through AI.
