Actual Size.

A picture-book colophon of the natural world, printed at one-to-one.

Steve Jenkins prints animals at real-world scale — a great white shark's tooth, a goliath bird-eating spider, a giant squid's eye — so a child's hand can lay flat against the page beside them.

Written and illustrated by Steve Jenkins.

Editorial Note
—  The Full Entry  —
What Makes It Orbital

A book that uses scale as its only argument. The premise needs no narration; the page is the experiment.

The Orbital Description

From there, the book opens onto more than one door. It is a math book about measurement and proportion. It is a geography book about the rainforests and oceans where these creatures live. It is a biology book about adaptation. The reader does the connecting — which is the whole point.

What Jenkins resists is interpretation. The text is small and factual, never narrating the wonder back to you — children form their own comparisons, and those comparisons are what stay.

On Reading

Best read together the first time, then left within the child's reach. Most children return to the comparison spreads on their own — the goliath spider, the squid's eye — and that quiet re-reading is where the connections form.

The reader does the connecting — which is the whole point.

Best For

For the child who already loves animals and is ready to be told the truth about how big a tongue, a tooth, an eye really is. For the child who is not yet sure whether she likes nonfiction. This book converts.
